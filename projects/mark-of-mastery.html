01 • Framing the project

Digital Game Project — Mark of Mastery

This project is a remake of my original university demo. I rebuilt it from scratch, keeping a few foundational ideas, but redesigning the experience to better match what I originally wanted it to feel like.

Why a remake?
The first demo proved the concept, but lacked the depth and fluidity I envisioned. This version focuses on combat variety, smarter enemies, and a clearer narrative direction for a potential full game.

03 • What’s changing

The big upgrades

The remake keeps the hack-and-slash core, but pushes depth in three directions: combat, enemy behavior, and story setup.

Upgrade 01

Combat overhaul

The original system worked, but didn’t have the fluidity I imagined. This remake aims for a more dynamic moveset with richer interactions and player expression.

Upgrade 02

Smarter enemy AI

Enemies should feel less scripted and more reactive. The goal is more varied encounters that require intentional play, not autopilot combos.

Upgrade 03

New story direction

The earlier demo was an intro without a clear end in mind. This remake uses the demo to set up a fuller narrative, with surface story + hidden discovery layers.

Even with these changes, the heart stays the same: high-energy action + narrative depth. This demo is also a learning playground — a chance to level up combat design, AI design, and storytelling craft.

04 • New systems

Mechanics added in the remake

Two early additions address clarity + flow: faster world traversal and a targeting system that feels reliable instead of disorienting.

Mechanic I

Sprinting

Added to reduce downtime when traveling between points. Simple, readable, and fast.

Mechanic II

Targeting

Rebuilt to avoid abrupt camera shifts and clearly communicate when you can lock-on.

Design note

Scope discipline

I considered a stamina bar (BOTW-style) but cut it for this demo to stay on schedule.

05 • Mechanic I

Sprinting (Out of combat / World)

In the previous demo, playtest feedback pointed out travel time between points felt slow. Sprinting fixes that with a lightweight addition: hold LEFT SHIFT while moving to sprint when unarmed.

06 • Mechanic II

Targeting (Combat)

The original targeting caused disorientation: the camera snapped hard to enemies, and players often chose to fight without lock-on. This remake rebuilds targeting around clear candidate states inspired by Kingdom Hearts.

State 01

Out of range

No target candidate — you’re too far for lock-on.

State 02

Candidate

In range — the game clearly indicates you can lock on.

State 03

Locked-on

Clear confirmation via UI highlight (healthbar border).

To keep both player and enemy in frame, targeting is constrained by range. If the player exits the sphere, the enemy removes itself as a candidate and breaks lock if active.
