Process Case Study

Mark of Mastery

A prototype exploring growth, mastery, and cinematic combat feel — built as a hands-on investigation into character design, camera language, UI clarity, and visual feedback.

Overview

Mark of Mastery began as an opportunity to bring one of my original characters into an interactive space, and evolved into a focused exploration of how presentation, camera movement, UI, and VFX can reinforce a player’s sense of progression and control.

Design Pillars

Character First

Designing a protagonist whose form, proportions, and expression feel readable and intentional.

Cinematic Camera

Using dolly, boom, tracking, and lock-on shots to elevate combat without sacrificing clarity.

Minimal UI

Reducing noise while clearly communicating health, stamina, and player options.

Expressive Feedback

Weapon trails, hit effects, and timing cues that make actions feel weighty and deliberate.

Process Highlights

Rather than building everything at once, development moved in focused passes — refining one layer of the experience before moving on to the next.

Development Gallery

Reflection & Takeaways

  • Strong visual feedback can dramatically improve perceived combat feel.
  • Camera movement must serve clarity first, spectacle second.
  • Iterative reduction often leads to stronger UI than additive design.
  • Process-driven exploration helped clarify my design instincts.
