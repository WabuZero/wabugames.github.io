01 • What this is

Overview

Mark of Mastery started as a character-forward action RPG prototype, but quickly became a focused experiment: how much “premium feel” can you get from strong presentation + readable systems?

Instead of building everything at once, I approached it in deliberate passes—tightening one layer (character, camera, UI, feedback) at a time until the whole experience felt cohesive.

02 • What guided decisions

Design Pillars

These pillars kept scope tight and ensured every change had a reason.

Pillar 01

Character Presence

Strong silhouette, intentional proportions, readable expression.

Pillar 02

Cinematic Camera

Dolly/boom/tracking choices that elevate without confusing.

Pillar 03

Minimal UI

Only what the player needs—fast reads, no clutter.

Pillar 04

Expressive Feedback

Trails, hit cues, and timing that make actions feel deliberate.

Pillar 05

Iterate in Passes

Improve one layer per iteration so results stay trackable.

Pillar 06

Clarity over Noise

If something looks cool but reduces readability, it gets reworked.

03 • How it was built

Process

Development moved through a clear sequence: get the shape right → get the camera right → get the UI right → polish feedback.

  • Character Pass
    Model/shape iterations to land a strong readable identity.
  • Camera Language
    Experimenting with framing choices to heighten feel without losing clarity.
  • UI Simplification
    Removing noise until the HUD felt “confident” and quick to parse.
  • Feedback & Polish
    Trails, hits, and timing cues aligned into one cohesive presentation.

05 • What I learned

Takeaways

Lesson

“Cool” must still read

VFX/camera choices only land when they don’t fight clarity.

Lesson

UI gets better by subtracting

The HUD improved most when I removed elements aggressively.

Lesson

Iterate in passes

Fix one layer at a time so each improvement stays measurable.
