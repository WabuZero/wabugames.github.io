High Road

A mystery-driven, emotionally charged platformer/stealth game where your choices tilt you between doubt and desire—rewiring routes, allies, and the story you tell yourself.

Overview

High Road explores how stealth and platforming can express inner conflict. Each route favors either desire (bold, high-risk lines) or doubt (careful, hidden paths), and the world remembers how you move. Encounters, dialogue, and level states reshape as you commit.

Design Hypothesis

Players feel the character’s tension most when traversal and stealth choices materially alter their future opportunities—without heavy cutscenes or exposition.

Win state for the prototype: deliver a short slice where two distinct play styles both feel valid, readable, and narratively meaningful.

Prototype Goals

  • Use traversal + stealth as implicit storytelling.
  • Build a core kit that supports “Doubt vs Desire” with distinct affordances.
  • Keep onboarding clear and frictionless; reward expressive mastery.
  • Ship a 5–8 minute vertical slice with reactive VO and stateful level changes.

Constraints

  • Indie scope; solo production and limited art bandwidth.
  • Timeboxed sprint for a milestone demo.
  • 1 environment + 3 encounter beats; clarity over content breadth.

Process

Mapping the Tension

System-map sessions turned “Doubt vs Desire” into level verbs, enemy vision cones, and traversal silhouettes. Paper grayboxes → Unity blockouts → weekly playtests.

Iteration Cadence

Each week targeted one “pain to pleasure” swap (e.g., readability, recovery options, stealth tells). Telemetry and quick interviews fed changes the same day.

Key Solutions

Affordance-First Lighting

Cold rim lights highlight doubt routes (cover, crawl), warm accents call out desire routes (vaults, wall-runs). Players learn the palette unconsciously.

Reactive Surfaces

Whispers on walls and reactive VO punctuate success/failure beats without stalling play. Surface lines shift tone as your route history skews one side.

Forgiving Fail-Forwards

Short, readable recovery ladders keep momentum after detection, keeping the fantasy intact for both stealth and flow-platforming players.

Readable Stealth Tells

Vision cones, footstep rings, and a “breath bar” reduce ambiguity while keeping pressure high.

Results & Takeaways

  • Players reliably recognized and chose between doubt/desire routes, and stuck with a style.
  • Heat-map paths confirmed the palette read: warm = bold, cold = hidden.
  • Biggest next step: deepen state carry-over and add one enemy archetype that flips the palette.
