Strife & Serenity

A narrative-driven action RPG about balance, consequence, and the cost of peace. You play as Ka’Sipho, navigating the Zothari’s authoritarian peace and the Conflict Entity’s reset—where every victory carries a price, and every resolution reshapes the natural order.

Overview

Strife & Serenity asks what happens when peace is forced into a universe that needs conflict to grow. The Zothari’s enforced order collides with the Entity of Conflict’s reset, and you’re caught in between—pushing a combat system that’s readable, expressive, and narratively loaded.

Design North Star

Make combat feel like conversation—abilities and counters that clearly “say” intent, with payoffs that echo the theme of balance (conflict → resolution).

Vertical slice goal: Gate Town traversal, a set-piece arena, and a climactic duel where mechanical clarity supports each story beat.

Project Goals

  • Readable, learnable combat with expressive ability kits (no button soup).
  • Encounters that escalate tension → choice → consequence without cutscene crutches.
  • UI that communicates state at a glance (ability bar, parry windows, telegraphs).
  • Ship a polished prologue that stands alone as a portfolio slice.

Constraints

  • Solo development; art bandwidth focused on clarity over spectacle.
  • One major boss encounter + 2 skirmishes in slice scope.
  • Target 60fps on mid-range laptops at 1080p.

Process

From Pillars → Pacing

Defined pillars (clarity, consequence, cadence) and mapped them to encounter loops: telegraph → commit → counter window → resolve → reposition. Paper prototypes → Unity blockouts → weekly playtests.

Iteration Cadence

Each pass targeted a pain point (unclear tells, camera drift, i-frame timing). Telemetry/heatmaps informed arena geometry, enemy timings, and cooldown tuning.

Key Systems

Ability Kit: Strife vs Serenity

Strife skills front-load risk/reward and stagger windows; Serenity skills emphasize timing, parries, and controlled recovery. Mixing both creates synergy and narrative resonance.

Readable Tells & Counters

Color/shape-coded telegraphs, intent-based hitstop (warning vs reward), consistent parry i-frames, and an ability bar that mirrors combat state without clutter.

Boss Phases with Moral Weight

“Duel of intents” where your style nudges the boss toward aggressive vs composed AI sets, tying player habit back into the theme of balance.

Traversal as Tone

Decompression spaces between fights seed worldbuilding via lighting, signage, and environmental storytelling—no exposition dumps necessary.

Results & Takeaways

  • Playtesters could read enemy intent and reliably parry within 2–3 attempts.
  • Ability synergies encouraged mixing Strife/Serenity rather than one-tricking.
  • Next: add a support archetype, deepen phase reactivity, and expand in-world onboarding.
