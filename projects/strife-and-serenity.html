Strife & Serenity

Narrative-driven Action RPG about balance, consequence, and the cost of peace.

Project Context

My Game Pitch

A narrative-driven action RPG about balance, consequence, and the cost of peace. You play as Ka’Sipho — a peacekeeper whose abilities evolve alongside his emotional arc, blending combat readability with thematic pacing.

Development Targets

Prototype Goals

  • Use combat pacing as implicit storytelling
  • Evolve the ability kit across emotional beats
  • Ensure first-time readability through clear telegraphs
  • Support expressive mastery without complexity bloat
Production Limits

Constraints

  • Solo-led production
  • Academic deadlines → frequent playable slices
  • Art bandwidth limitations → stylized UI first
  • Timeboxed iteration cycles

Process

1. Research & Mood

  • Mapped desired cadence (pressure → decision → catharsis)
  • Studied readable combat: silhouettes, telegraphs, hit-pause, subtle screenshake

2. Prototype & Playtest

  • Fast iteration cycles for movement, camera, and kit timings
  • Weekly playtests—tracked readability, fail states, encounter beats

3. Scope & Polish

  • Cut feature creep; doubled down on clarity + feel
  • Added narrative surfaces (barks, pickups) that respect flow

Key Solutions

1) Emotion-Linked Ability Kit

My thought process

  • Let the kit mirror Ka’Sipho’s internal state so mechanics carry narrative
  • Gate complexity behind emotional milestones, not just levels

Results

  • Progression “feels earned” and readable; avoids info-dumping
  • Playtesters reported stronger attachment to moment-to-moment choices

2) Readable Combat Language

My thought process

  • Prioritize silhouettes, clear windups, generous hit-pauses
  • Keep HUD minimal; communicate through animation + VFX where possible

Results

  • Higher first-time clarity; fewer cheap hits in playtests
  • Players focused on space, timing, and intent—not UI

3) Encounter Pacing as Story

My thought process

  • Structure beats to echo the theme: strife → insight → serenity
  • Use camera assists and arena shape language to guide attention

Results

  • Smoother rhythm; moments feel like “earned catharsis”
  • Reduced difficulty spikes by smoothing recovery windows

Takeaways

  • Clarity first: fewer, sharper mechanics > many half-baked ones
  • Emotional arc + readable kit progression keeps players engaged
  • Short loops and decisive cuts protect scope and momentum
