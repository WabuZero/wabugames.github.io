Strife & Serenity

Narrative-driven Action RPG about balance, consequence, and the cost of peace.

Features

  • Readable, tactile combat with encounter pacing as storytelling
  • Emotion-linked ability kit that evolves with the narrative arc
  • Lore surfaces and reactive VO that punctuate play without stalling

Goals

  • Make players feel the tension → release loop through mechanics
  • Keep onboarding approachable while supporting expressive mastery
  • Ship small playable slices on a consistent cadence

Constraints

  • Indie scope; solo-led production with limited art bandwidth
  • Unity prototype; emphasis on clarity over content breadth
  • Timeboxed milestones across academic + personal schedule

Process

1. Research & Mood

  • Mapped desired emotions and cadence (pressure → decision → catharsis)
  • Studied readable combat: silhouettes, telegraphs, hit-pause/screen-shake

2. Prototype & Playtest

  • Fast iteration cycles for movement, camera, and core kit timings
  • Weekly playtests—tracked readability, fail states, and encounter beats

3. Scope & Polish

  • Cut feature creep; doubled down on clarity and feel
  • Added small narrative surfaces (barks, pickups) that respect flow

Key Solutions

1) Emotion-Linked Ability Kit

My thought process

  • Let the kit mirror Ka’Sipho’s internal state so mechanics carry narrative
  • Gate complexity behind emotional milestones, not just level gates

Results

  • Progression “feels earned” and readable; avoids info-dumping
  • Playtesters reported stronger attachment to moment-to-moment choices

2) Readable Combat Language

My thought process

  • Prioritise silhouettes, clear windups, and generous hit-pauses
  • Keep HUD minimal; communicate through animation and VFX where possible

Results

  • Higher first-time clarity; fewer cheap hits in playtests
  • Players kept focus on space, timing, and intent—not UI

3) Encounter Pacing as Story

My thought process

  • Structure beats to echo the theme: strife → insight → serenity
  • Use camera assists and arena shape language to guide attention

Results

  • Better rhythm; players described moments as “earned catharsis”
  • Reduced difficulty spikes by smoothing recovery windows

Takeaways

  • Clarity first: fewer, sharper mechanics > many half-baked ones
  • Emotional arc + readable kit progression keeps players engaged
  • Short loops and decisive cuts protect scope and momentum
