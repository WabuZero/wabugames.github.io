Overview

The prologue introduces the world at a moment of fragile peace and rising tension—no spoilers, just stakes you can feel through play. The slice focuses on clear telegraphs, satisfying counters, and traversal that breathes between encounters.

What’s in this slice

  • Gate Town traversal: onboarding, readable affordances, and tone-setting.
  • Set-piece arena: waves that teach timing, spacing, and recovery options.
  • Boss-style duel: “conversation” combat—telegraph → commit → counter → resolve.

Play the current prologue build on Itch.io and learn more at WabuGames.com.

North Star: Combat should communicate intent like dialogue—every tell and counter reads instantly, and every payoff reinforces the theme of balance without stating it outright.

Project Goals (Prologue)

  • Deliver a 5–8 minute prologue that stands alone.
  • Prioritize readability (tells, counters, UI states) over content breadth.
  • Use traversal pacing to create tension → release between fights.
  • Teach the ability kit through play, not exposition.

Constraints

  • Solo production → focus on clarity, lighting, and UI polish over bespoke art volume.
  • 1 boss-style duel, 2 skirmish beats, 1 traversal lane.
  • Target: 60fps @ 1080p on mid-range laptops.

Process

From Pillars → Encounters

Pillars (clarity, consequence, cadence) mapped to loops: telegraph → commit → counter window → resolve → reposition. Paper → blockout → weekly playtests with quick tuning passes.

Iteration

Addressed one friction per pass (camera drift, parry window, tell contrast, recovery routes). Heatmaps + interview notes informed arena shape and ability cooldowns.

Key Systems (Prologue)

Ability Kit: Strife / Serenity

Strife skills emphasize assertive windows and risk/reward; Serenity skills focus on timing, parries, and controlled recovery. Mixing both creates satisfying decision loops.

Readable Tells & Counters

Color/shape-coded telegraphs, intent-based hitstop, consistent i-frames, and an ability bar that mirrors combat state at a glance.

Traversal as Tone

Quiet spaces between fights shift lighting and signage to seed context and decompress without cutscenes.

Fail-Forward Recovery

Short, visible recovery ladders keep momentum after mistakes so players stay in the fantasy.

Results & Takeaways

  • Playtesters reliably read enemy intent and landed parries within 2–3 attempts.
  • Players mixed Strife/Serenity skills instead of one-tricking, thanks to clear affordances.
  • Next steps: add one support archetype, deepen duel phase reactivity, and expand in-world onboarding beats.
