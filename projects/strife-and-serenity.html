Seeds of Conflict

Hunt a trusted ally turned traitor whose actions stoke a realm on the brink of war. Track their influence, sever the Conflict Entity’s reach, and keep fragile peace from collapsing.

Core Gameplay Loop

  • Action: Fight corrupted / conflicted enemies and mini-bosses.
  • Reward: Earn loot, upgrade materials, items, and unlock new abilities.
  • Opportunity: Meet odd characters, uncover lore, and see how conflict shapes the world. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Target Audience

  • Age: 16–35 — themes of betrayal, loyalty, and conflict resonate with older teens & adults.
  • Inclusive: Designed for players of all races, genders, and backgrounds.
  • Lifestyle: Students, part-time workers, and young professionals with time to play.
  • Platform: PC first (fidelity + flexibility); consider consoles post-launch. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Combat

Flashy, responsive action where readable telegraphs and honest counters make payoffs feel earned. Attacks fall into distinct categories to keep rhythm and variety high. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Mechanics — Marks

Core systems revolve around “Marks” that modulate ability windows, damage routes, and counter opportunities. Stacking and timing Marks rewards deliberate play without sacrificing flow. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Cutscenes — Camera Work

Dialogue advances with mostly fixed framing, punctuated by purposeful moves: Tilt, Zoom, Pan, Boom, and Truck for emphasis and clarity. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Cutscenes — Artwork

High-impact scenes shift from in-engine models to illustrated cut-ins for expressiveness. Dialogue sprites change with emotion to deepen character voice. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

UI / UX

Readability first: attack types, states, and cooldowns surface clearly; players always understand what just happened and what they can do next. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

A restless prodigy chasing a brother-figure turned enemy; flippant surface, purpose underneath. His pursuit tests loyalty, justice, and identity. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}
